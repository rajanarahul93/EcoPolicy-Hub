Understanding Climate Policies

Explore key global and national policies designed to combat climate change and reduce carbon emissions.

Why Climate Policies Matter

Climate policies play a crucial role in mitigating the effects of global warming by regulating emissions, promoting sustainable practices, and encouraging green technologies.

Global Climate Agreements

  • Paris Agreement (2015): A legally binding international treaty aiming to limit global warming to well below 2°C.
  • Kyoto Protocol (1997): An earlier agreement that set binding emission reduction targets for developed countries.
  • Montreal Protocol (1987): A successful treaty focused on phasing out substances that deplete the ozone layer.

Climate Policies in India

  • National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC): India’s strategy to promote clean energy, water conservation, and sustainable agriculture.
  • Perform, Achieve & Trade (PAT) Scheme: A market-based mechanism to improve energy efficiency in large industries.
  • Renewable Energy Targets: India aims to reach 500 GW of renewable energy capacity by 2030.

How You Can Contribute

Climate policies are effective only when individuals take action. Here’s how you can help:

  • Support sustainable businesses and products.
  • Reduce energy consumption by using efficient appliances.
  • Advocate for stronger environmental policies in your community.
  • Adopt a low-carbon lifestyle by minimizing waste and using public transport.
